Roofing Basics: An Overview
What is Roofing?: Roofing is the process of covering the top of a building to protect it from weather elements, ensuring safety, insulation, and comfort.
Types of Roofs: Roofs can be residential or commercial, with variations like pitched (sloped) roofs for homes and flat roofs for commercial buildings. For pitched roofing, RHIVE’s manufacturer is Owens Corning. For commercial roofing, we use GAF as our manufacturer for PVC and membrane systems.
Roof Anatomy: Key components of a roof include the decking (base structure), underlayment (protective layer), shingles (outer covering), flashing (waterproofing edges), and vents (for ventilation). For flat roofing systems, additional components include PVC or TPO membrane layers, insulation boards for thermal resistance, and ballast or adhesive systems to secure the membrane.
Roofing Materials and Their Functions
Asphalt Shingles: The most common roofing material; affordable and durable, available in a variety of styles. It is important to note that asphalt shingles are a water-shedding system, not a waterproof system. The granules embedded in the tar act as a protective layer to shed water effectively, rather than forming a completely watertight barrier.
Metal Roofing: Durable and fire-resistant, often used for its long lifespan and energy efficiency.
Tile, Slate, and Wood Shakes: Premium materials known for their aesthetics, durability, and specific design styles.
PVC and Membrane Roofing: Commonly used for flat roofs, providing durability, flexibility, and strong water resistance, especially suitable for commercial applications.
Roof Underlayment: A water-resistant layer between the decking and the shingles that protects against water infiltration.
Flashing and Vents: Flashing seals roof joints and edges to prevent leaks, while vents regulate attic temperature and airflow.
Roof Installation Process
Valley Installation Techniques: In roofing, valleys can be constructed using different methods to direct water off the roof efficiently. Two common methods are the California Cut and the Woven Valley.
California Cut: At RHIVE Construction, we only use the California Cut method. This method involves cutting the shingles to create a clean line down the valley, providing a smooth, aesthetically pleasing finish. It is often used because it is relatively straightforward to install and provides reliable water shedding.
Woven Valley: In a woven valley, shingles from both sides of the valley are interlaced, creating a continuous weave. This method offers excellent water resistance and adds a visually distinct pattern to the valley.
 
Roof Installation Process
Tear-Off: At RHIVE Construction, the installation process for asphalt roofing always begins with tearing off the old roof materials down to the decking. RHIVE does not do layovers, as this allows us to properly inspect the decking for any damage that may need repair before installing new materials. Layovers are like a band-aid solution, covering existing problems rather than addressing them directly. Additionally, layovers typically only provide half the lifespan of what the roofing system can achieve when installed correctly from the decking up.
Preparation and Inspection: Inspect roof decking, If any sheets of OSB are found to be damaged or deteriorating, they must be replaced to ensure a strong foundation for the roofing system. Additionally, identify any slat board decking, which is often seen in older homes. Slat board decking may need reinforcement or replacement to meet modern standards. Measure and calculate material needs, and ensure all safety protocols are followed.
Underlayment Installation: Lay the underlayment across the decking. This protective layer serves as a secondary barrier against water infiltration.
Stages of Shingle Installation:
Starter Shingles: Begin with installing starter shingles along the eaves. These shingles create a foundation for the main shingles and help prevent wind uplift at the roof's edge.
Main Shingles (Body): Install the main asphalt shingles over the underlayment, staggering them to ensure a watertight seal and optimal coverage. The main shingles form the bulk of the roof and provide the primary weather barrier.
Hip and Ridge Shingles: Finish by installing hip and ridge shingles along the roof's ridgelines and hips. These shingles provide a polished look and additional protection to the roof's most vulnerable areas.
Flashing and Vents: Install flashing around roof penetrations such as chimneys and vents to ensure water does not seep into gaps. Add ridge vents and other ventilation components to ensure proper attic airflow.
Safety Guidelines: Key safety practices include using harnesses, working in pairs, and using protective gear like gloves and helmets.
Roofing Systems and Warranties
What is a Roofing System?: A complete roofing system includes several integrated components like shingles, underlayment, and ventilation to ensure durability and efficiency.
Warranties: Roofing warranties can include manufacturer warranties on materials and workmanship warranties on the quality of the installation. At RHIVE Construction, we only use manufacturer-approved components to uphold these warranties. Unlike other contractors who may mix and match materials to save money, which can void the manufacturer’s warranty, RHIVE ensures that every component used is compliant with the manufacturer's requirements, ensuring the integrity and longevity of your roof.
Common Roofing Problems and Solutions
Leaks and Water Damage: Often caused by missing shingles, improper installation, or damaged flashing. Prevented by regular inspections, prompt repairs, and replacement.
Ventilation Issues: Poor ventilation can cause moisture buildup and damage. Proper installation of ridge and soffit vents can prevent these issues.
Shingle Deterioration: Age, UV exposure, and severe weather can cause shingle wear and tear. Proper maintenance and timely replacement are essential.
